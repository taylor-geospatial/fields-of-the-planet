# Pre-submission review: Fields of the Planet (FTP)

Review target: WACV 2027 paper in `paper/main.tex` plus figure/table generation code and `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md`.

## 1. Blocking errors

1. Main and supplement are currently one 21-page review PDF. WACV 2027 author guidelines say papers over eight pages excluding references are rejected without review (https://wacv.thecvf.com/Conferences/2027/AuthorGuides). The source enters the appendix directly after references at `paper/main.tex:610`, while the existing build log reports `Output written on main.pdf (21 pages, 9346868 bytes).` at `paper/main.log:717`. Fix: split the appendix into supplemental material, using the style helper at `paper/wacv.sty:502`-`paper/wacv.sty:512` (`\maketitlesupplementary`) or a separate supplement file, and keep the main paper through references within the WACV limit.

2. The paper claims raw UDM2 masks are released, but provenance says they are not retained. `paper/main.tex:70` says `133,168 SR images and UDM2 masks`; `paper/main.tex:771` lists `UDM2 masks (8-band, uint8) & 133,168`; `paper/main.tex:603` says FTP releases `UDM2 quality masks`. `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:60` says the table is re-derived from `data/planet/index.parquet` and "the raw UDM2 tifs are not retained." Fix one side: either ship the UDM2 mask files and paths, or change the paper to "UDM2 QA statistics / quality fields" everywhere and replace the `tab:scope` row with `UDM2 QA-stat windows (non-null) & 129,490` or `UDM2 QA fields for SR windows & 133,168`.

3. UDM2 denominators are wrong in prose/caption. `paper/main.tex:113` says statistics are across the full `133k UDM2 release` and quotes `89.7%`; `paper/main.tex:787` says percentages are of `133k (patch, window) tiles`; `paper/main.tex:801` repeats `% of (patch, window) tiles`. But `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:60` defines the denominator as `dropna()` over UDM2 stats, `n=129,490 tiles`. Fix: change `paper/main.tex:113` and `paper/main.tex:787` to "129,490 released windows with non-null UDM2 stats"; keep `89.7%`. If the intended denominator is all 133,168 released windows, recompute the usable fraction as `87.2%` and update the table/prose.

4. The upsampled-S2 control mixes boundary-error conventions and contradicts the headline table. The headline caption says Sentinel-2 boundary chamfer is reported at each model's native grid at `paper/main.tex:231`; the generator enforces that at `paper/scripts/polygon_metrics_table.py:32`-`paper/scripts/polygon_metrics_table.py:36`, producing S2-B3 boundary `18.6 / 54.7` at `paper/figs/polygon_metrics.tex:11`. But `paper/main.tex:448` and `paper/main.tex:966` report the same `Upsampled at eval` S2-B3 row with boundary `31.3 / 96.9`, and `paper/main.tex:954`-`paper/main.tex:955` says the row is reproduced from `tab:polygon_metrics`. Fix: for the `Upsampled at eval` row, use native-grid boundary `18.6` mean and `54.7` p95 to match `tab:polygon_metrics`, then rewrite `paper/main.tex:420`-`paper/main.tex:425` and `paper/main.tex:934`-`paper/main.tex:945` so only the `Trained upsampled` row is described as `28.0m`. Alternative: explicitly label the control table boundary columns as upsampled-grid chamfer and stop calling that row reproduced/headline.

5. Per-region figure scripts use a newer 23-region Planet CSV than the paper text/provenance says, so rerunning figures changes the quoted numbers. `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:31`-`paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:35` says 23-region figures/prose use `logs/polygon_metrics/planet_b3_augmax_full_22.csv`, giving the quoted `37.9` macro and old per-region deltas. The current paper quotes the old values at `paper/main.tex:468`-`paper/main.tex:480` and `paper/main.tex:493`-`paper/main.tex:499`; the figure captions repeat them at `paper/main.tex:566` and `paper/main.tex:905`-`paper/main.tex:917`. Fix: change the 23-region figure scripts back to `logs/polygon_metrics/planet_b3_augmax_full_22.csv` and update `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:44` and `paper/scripts/PROVENANCE.md:46` accordingly. Fix: replace all of these with a shared constant-divisor renderer, e.g. `np.clip(rgb.astype(np.float32) / 3000.0, 0.0, 1.0)`, same divisor across channels, then regenerate the PDFs.

Fix: expand first uses as "surface-reflectance (SR)", "Usable Data Mask 2 (UDM2)", "area of interest (AOI)", "watershed (WS) + test-time augmentation (TTA)", and "Cloud-Optimized GeoTIFF (COG)."
